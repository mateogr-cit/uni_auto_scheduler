Notes from reading the current schema:
  • course_schedule and its child course_session both carry room_id, slot_id, and session_type_id. The auto-scheduler creates one CourseSchedule per (course, group) and two CourseSession children (Lecture and Seminar), so the columns on the parent are effectively redundant.
  • prof and student are subtype tables sharing their primary key with user. The PK·FK badge marks that single column, and the crow's foot side reads as "zero or one" because a given user may or may not be promoted into a subtype.
  • professor_course is a pure junction (no surrogate id); its composite PK is both columns at once.
  • student.group_id is nullable, so its line carries the "zero or one" symbol on the student_group side rather than the usual "one and only one".
  • resolution_log has no foreign-key lines on purpose: it stores a JSON snapshot in summary so the source complaint or unavailability row can be deleted later without breaking the audit trail.
